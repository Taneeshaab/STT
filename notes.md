My model leverages prosodic signals—specifically RMS energy decay, spectral flatness, and fundamental frequency (F0) slopes—to detect turn-ends. I utilized Delta and Delta-Delta features to model the velocity and acceleration of speech roll-off, which are stronger indicators of EOT than silence alone. Silence-only baselines fail because they cannot distinguish between cognitive pauses ("holds") and actual turn transitions. During development, I identified a specific failure mode: "backchanneling" (users making small agreements like "mhm" during pauses). I addressed this by incorporating spectral flatness, allowing the classifier to differentiate between background agreement and true conversation ends. The model currently optimizes for the 5% interruption constraint through probability threshold calibration. Its main weakness remains unexpected non-speech noise, which can mask the subtle pitch drops needed for accurate detection. With one more day, I would add MFCCs for better phonetic modeling and experiment with a lightweight RNN to process the temporal sequence of the pause.
